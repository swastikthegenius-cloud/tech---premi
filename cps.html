About CPS testing

CPS means clicks per second. The score is found by counting how many times you click during the test and dividing that by the amount of time used. If you click 50 times in 10 seconds, your CPS is 5.

Short tests usually give higher numbers because people can spam faster for a few seconds before their hand slows down. Longer tests are better for checking steady speed instead of burst speed.

Your mouse, finger position, desk height, and even how springy the button feels can change the result. Some people use one finger, some use butterfly clicking, and some switch hands when they get tired.

A high CPS does not always mean better aim or better gaming skill. It mostly shows how quickly you can repeat the same action in a short time without stopping.

If your speed changes a lot between tries, that is normal. Hand fatigue, rhythm, focus, and even excitement can make a difference from one round to the next.
